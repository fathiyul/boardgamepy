# DixiQuote — Situation Deck (Revised)

*80 situation cards rewritten to consistently evoke imagery while preserving interpretive openness.*

Each card describes a concrete scene or event with sensory or narrative anchors. Meaning is not stated; it is suggested. The text is intentionally long enough to trigger imagination, but short enough to remain flexible.

---

## Fantasy & Myth (20)

1. The dragon circles the city once, its shadow passing over every tower, then disappears into the clouds without attacking.
2. Just as the warning bells fall silent, a giant worm breaks the ground beneath the outer wall and retreats again.
3. The sword that has never been drawn is finally removed from its sheath, and nothing immediately changes.
4. As the wizard completes the spell, they realize another voice has been speaking the words with them.
5. Years later, someone notices that the prophecy was fulfilled during an ordinary afternoon.
6. The crown fits perfectly when placed on the pedestal, but the hall is empty when the doors are opened.
7. The final spell in the ancient book is written in handwriting that looks familiar to everyone.
8. On the day the temple is completed, the gods stop answering prayers.
9. The monster lowers its head and bows just before it is struck down.
10. The forest parts to reveal a path that none of the maps have ever shown.
11. When summoned, the familiar spirit appears briefly and then refuses to return again.
12. The knight rides into the battlefield to find the banners fallen and the fighting over.
13. The ancient door unlocks itself slowly, but no one steps through.
14. The oracle gives a clear answer, and only later does it become clear it was meant for someone else.
15. The city holds a victory feast for a battle that never took place.
16. Giant footprints cross the valley, but they fade in a direction that makes no sense.
17. The magical artifact works exactly once, then becomes an ordinary object.
18. Standing at the edge of home, the hero realizes the journey back feels longer than the quest itself.
19. When the curse lifts, the scars remain visible on everyone who lived through it.
20. During the coronation, the throne remains empty while the crowd waits.

## Science Fiction (20)

21. The space station receives a distress signal dated twenty years in the future, repeating every hour.
22. The time traveler comes face to face with a version of themselves who does not recognize them.
23. The clone wakes up with a full set of memories that clearly belong to someone else.
24. Asked a routine question, the AI pauses for several seconds before responding.
25. The final message from Earth arrives after the colony has already stopped listening.
26. An android is publicly thanked for an act it never performed.
27. Without issuing any warning, the ship quietly alters its course.
28. The experiment succeeds, but the collected data contradicts itself line by line.
29. The planet appears completely uninhabited until the lights turn on at night.
30. The return signal is much weaker than the transmission that was sent.
31. While reviewing the logs, the crew notices the star map has been updating on its own.
32. A portal opens to a place that shows clear signs of having been abandoned long ago.
33. The simulation ends, but no one removes their headset.
34. The backup system activates while the original system continues running.
35. The explorer arrives at coordinates that no longer exist on any chart.
36. A message is received in a language that has not yet been invented.
37. The ship’s log records an event that no crew member remembers experiencing.
38. The countdown reaches zero, pauses, and then continues into negative numbers.
39. After months of work, the inventor deletes the only functioning prototype.
40. The colony votes to remain offline despite having full communication restored.

## Surreal & Symbolic (20)

41. Overnight, the river changes its course, yet every map still shows the old path.
42. Each time the room is empty, the statue’s head is turned slightly in a new direction.
43. The sun rises a little earlier each day, and no one mentions it.
44. A new door appears in a room that was sealed the night before.
45. Looking into the mirror reveals a moment from years ago instead of a reflection.
46. The shadow enters the room several seconds before the person does.
47. The clock continues ticking long after its hands have fallen off.
48. A letter is delivered carefully, even though it has no address.
49. The sound stops suddenly, but the echo continues.
50. Walking through the house makes it clear that it remembers being full.
51. A road leads somewhere different each time it is followed.
52. The painting is considered finished only when the artist stops looking at it.
53. For a brief moment, the rain falls upward.
54. A name is spoken clearly, but no one remembers learning it.
55. After the furniture is removed, the room feels noticeably smaller.
56. The message is perfectly clear, but its meaning remains uncertain.
57. The page is blank except for a single underline waiting to be filled.
58. Long after the bell is removed, it rings once.
59. The footprints end suddenly in the middle of an empty field.
60. The light stays on even after the building is completely abandoned.

## Epic, Social, & Historical (20)

61. Just before the last warrior falls, the flag is raised one final time above the hill.
62. By morning, the border has moved, and familiar roads now belong to another country.
63. The messenger arrives breathless, only to learn the war ended days earlier.
64. The city finishes preparing for a disaster that never comes.
65. The vote passes unanimously, and then slowly begins to unravel in practice.
66. The celebration continues even after no one remembers what started it.
67. Giving the order, the general already knows it will be ignored.
68. The archive carefully preserves the original mistake instead of the correction.
69. The monument is built facing away from the event it commemorates.
70. The story spreads faster and farther than the event itself ever did.
71. The witness is believed completely, but for the wrong reason.
72. The council adjourns as night falls, having reached no conclusion.
73. The parade moves through streets that no longer exist on current maps.
74. The promise is fulfilled long after it has stopped mattering to anyone.
75. The announcement is met with polite applause and long silence.
76. A long-standing record is broken, then quietly restored the next day.
77. The ceremony ends before most people realize it has begun.
78. Overnight, the border markers are repainted without explanation.
79. During revisions, a name is carefully removed from the official list.
80. The banner is folded away as the crowd slowly disperses.

*These situations are designed to be framed, remembered, and reinterpreted. The meaning is never on the card.*
